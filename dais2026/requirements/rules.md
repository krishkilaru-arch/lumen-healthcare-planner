## 4. How To Enter

### 4.1 Entry Process

After registering for the Data + AI Summit, prospective Entrants may enter to participate in the Hackathon by visiting the Data + AI Summit website, completing the Application Form, and following the below steps.

Not all prospective Entrants who complete an Application Form will be invited to participate. Responses will be evaluated based on:

* Quality and completeness of Registration Form
* Demonstrated relevant experience
* The prospective Entrant's statement of motivation for participating
* Cohort balancing to ensure a balance of functional skills among Entrants

Steps:

1. Apply to be considered as a Hackathon participant.
2. Upon successful registration, Entrants will receive an email with their registration status by June 1, 2026.
3. Before the start of the Hackathon, Entrants will receive an email containing key instructions.
4. Entrants will obtain access to the required developer tools and complete a project described in "Project Requirements."
5. If you have formed a team prior to checking in, your team's Representative must register your team via the Devpost link. If you intend to form a team with assistance, this will be done after check-in.
6. Once a team is registered, the Representative must complete and enter all required fields on Devpost (each a "Submission") during the Project Period.

### 4.2 Project Requirements

* **How to access the required tools:** Eligible Teams will use Databricks Free Edition for the event.
* **What to Create:** Create a Databricks App built on Lakebase and using one or more additional Databricks tools.
* **Platforms:** Databricks for building the projects, Devpost to submit projects and form teams.
* **New Projects Only:** Projects must be created only during the Project Period.
* **Third Party Integrations:** Entrants may use provided datasets, but this is not a requirement. They can also use outside models to train with Databricks.

### 4.3 Submission Requirements

Submissions must:

1. Include a project built with the required developer tools that meets the Project Requirements.
2. Provide access to an Entrant's working project for judging and testing by providing a link to a website, functioning demo, or a test build. If private, include login credentials.
3. Include a text description that explains the features and functionality of your project.
4. Include a URL to your public and open-source GitHub repository. The repository must show that the project was built during the Project Period, include an open-source license, and be publicly visible.
5. Include a demonstration video of your project:
   * Should be no longer than three (3) minutes
   * Should include footage that shows the project functioning on the device for which it was built
   * Must be uploaded to and made publicly visible on YouTube, Vimeo, Facebook Video, or Youku
   * Must not include third-party intellectual property without permission
6. Be the original work of the members of the Eligible Team, be solely owned by them, and must not plagiarize any third-party works.
7. Each Eligible Team may submit only one Submission, and each Entrant may only join one Eligible Team.

### 4.4 Language Requirements

All Submission materials must be in English or, if not in English, the Entrant must provide an English translation of the demonstration video, text description, and testing instructions.

### 4.5 Team Representation

If an Eligible Team is entering, it must appoint and authorize one Representative to represent it, enter a Submission and act on its behalf.

### 4.6 Intellectual Property in the Submission

Your Submission must: (a) be your Eligible Team's original work product; (b) be solely owned by your Eligible Team; and (c) not violate the intellectual property rights or other rights of any other person or entity.

An Eligible Team may contract with a third party for technical assistance, provided the Submission components are solely your Eligible Team's work product. An Eligible Team may submit a Submission that includes the use of open source software or hardware, provided the Eligible Team complies with applicable open source licenses and creates software that enhances and builds upon the underlying open source product.

### 4.7 Prohibited Content

Submissions must be suitable for publication and viewing by general audiences and must not include any content that is harmful, illegal or indecent.

### 4.8 Financial or Preferential Support

Projects that have been entered into other competitions and/or are already being commercialized or funded by a third party are prohibited. A project must not have been developed with financial or preferential support from the Sponsor or Administrator.

## 6. Judges & Criteria

Eligible Submissions will be evaluated by a panel of judges selected by the Sponsor (the "Judges"). Judges may be composed of employees of the Sponsor or third parties (at least one judge will be an independent judge).

### Stage One (Pass/Fail)

Determines whether the Submission has provided all necessary Submission Requirements:
* GitHub repository URL is working and publicly accessible
* Demonstration video does not exceed 3 minutes
* Demo/test build is accessible publicly or via login credentials

### Stage Two (Judging Criteria — equally weighted)

1. **Business Applicability** — How well does this solve a real business problem for your industry?
2. **Data Relevance** — How have you combined relevant and interesting Databricks datasets and tools?
3. **Creativity** — Is this a new and original idea, or has this been done before?
4. **Thoroughness** — Is your application easy for the end user to understand? Does it provide relevant and insightful information?
5. **Well-Architected** — Can your application/RAG model scale well at a linear cost? Can it accommodate additional features without rewriting most of the codebase?

### Winners

* **First Prize:** Highest overall scores
* **Second Prize:** Second highest overall scores
* **Third Prize:** Third highest overall scores

**Tie Breaking:** The tied Submission with the highest score in the first applicable criterion will be considered higher scoring. If ties remain, the process repeats on the next criterion. If still tied on all criteria, the panel of Judges will vote.
